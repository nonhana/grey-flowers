# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Grey Flowers is for readers who move between programming practice and personal reflection. The primary audience is Chinese-speaking developers, peers, and curious visitors who want to read articles, browse notes, understand the author behind the work, and return to a site that feels personal rather than industrial.

They usually arrive to read long-form writing, skim recent activity, inspect code-oriented material, or decide whether they want to follow the author's work. The interface should help them settle in quickly, read comfortably, and feel that there is a coherent person behind the writing.

## Product Purpose

Grey Flowers is a personal publishing garden. It documents technical learning, software practice, self-exploration, and everyday traces in one place.

Success is not measured by aggressive conversion behavior. Success means the site feels memorable, readable, and trustworthy, the writing remains the center of gravity, and visitors can move between articles, thoughts, recent activity, and personal context without losing the sense of a single authored world.

## Brand Personality

Reflective, disciplined, gentle.

The voice should feel like a careful developer keeping a personal notebook in public. It should be calm without becoming flat, technical without becoming sterile, and poetic without drifting into ornament for its own sake.

The emotional goal is quiet confidence. Readers should feel invited into a cultivated space, not pushed through a funnel.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI SaaS landing-page cliches.
- Neon cyberpunk dev-blog dark mode.
- Glassmorphism-heavy personal portfolio.
- Hard-edged enterprise dashboard chrome.

## Design Principles

- Let writing lead. The interface exists to support reading, orientation, and memory, not to compete with the content.
- Keep the garden metaphor structural. Use it as atmosphere, pacing, and naming, not as decoration pasted onto every surface.
- Pair technical clarity with personal warmth. Navigation, metadata, and controls should stay legible and precise while the overall world remains human.
- Favor continuity over spectacle. Motion, accent color, and state changes should reward attention without turning the site into a performance.
- Make every section feel authored. Articles, about pages, recent activity, and dialogs should belong to one system rather than separate templates.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard usability across both light and dark themes. Reading comfort takes priority: stable line height, clear focus states, semantic headings, and restrained motion that never blocks content comprehension.

The site should remain usable for visitors reading in zh-Hans and English contexts, for people browsing on narrow mobile screens, and for readers who prefer dark mode. Animated reveals should stay brief and optional in effect, not essential to understanding the page.
